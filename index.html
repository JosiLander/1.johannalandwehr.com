About

In practice that means negotiating at decision-maker level with international clients and regulators, building durable long-term relationships, and acting as the central link between external stakeholders and internal cross-functional teams, including strategic updates at VP level.

What I bring

Three things I do that move IT and partnership organizations forward.

01

Steering global partner & stakeholder networks under delivery pressure

Cross-Border Stakeholder Management

IT and partnership organizations scale through exactly this pattern: many autonomous external players — vendors, system integrators, tech partners — synchronized across time zones, jurisdictions and compliance regimes onto one shared delivery date. I've run that for 12+ parallel global programs across 15+ countries, as the central interface between clients and internal teams up to VP level.

Business Impact
Less friction in partner onboarding, more robust governance during international expansion, lower escalation risk into senior management — carried over from an environment where a mistake doesn't mean "ticket reopened" but patient care.
02

Translating between business needs and technical delivery

Requirements Engineering / Tech-Business Bridge

The most expensive gap in IT and partnership projects sits between what business and partners ask for and what engineering builds. Whoever speaks both languages fluently prevents friction, misinterpretation and scope creep right there. I translate complex requirements into robust functional and technical specs on an engineering foundation, with hybrid (agile + classic) project management.

Business Impact
Fewer rework hours, shorter time-to-delivery, sharper requirement documents as the basis for robust partner and client contracts — and therefore lower risk of costly scope disputes.
03

Risk-driven financial governance instead of reactive project controlling

P&L Responsibility / Proactive Risk Management

IT projects and partnerships rarely fail on technology — they fail on late-spotted risk, unclear escalation paths and budget overruns that only surface in the status meeting. I treat risk and budget as live control variables, not a monthly report: full ownership of budget, revenue forecasting and invoicing, backed by KPI reporting and Power BI rather than gut feeling — which let me actively prevent four critical escalations.

Business Impact
Margin protection on multi-million projects, more reliable forecasts for management and investor reporting, measurably fewer costly last-minute escalations — and therefore higher partner trust in your delivery reliability.
